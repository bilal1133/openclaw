# TkTurners Business Plan (Ecommerce Automation Niche)
**Document owner:** TkTurners Leadership  
**Date:** February 18, 2026  
**Primary audience:** Internal leadership and operators  
**Planning horizon:** 12-month execution plan + 24-month productization roadmap

## 1. Executive Summary
TkTurners is repositioning from a generic software and automation house to a niche ecommerce systems and revenue automation partner for US omnichannel retail brands.

The core thesis is simple: most ecommerce businesses do not have a sales problem alone. They have fragmented systems across storefronts, ERPs, payment gateways, and channels. This creates data delays, failed handoffs, payment leakage, and missed growth opportunities. TkTurners will solve this by integrating commerce systems end-to-end, then deploying automations and predictive workflows that increase sales performance.

Year 1 operating outcomes:
- Revenue scenarios: Base `$500k`, Target `$750k`, Stretch `$1M`.
- Core commercial model: fixed implementation sprint + recurring optimization retainer + optional AI modules.
- Delivery capacity target: `24-36` implementation sprints per year.
- Recurring base target: `8-15` active retainers.
- Credibility motion: `3` measurable pilot case studies in first 6 months.

## 3. Problem Landscape in Ecommerce Operations
| Problem area | Typical symptom | Business impact |
|---|---|---|
| Data fragmentation | ERP, storefront, and payment data do not match in near real-time | Delayed decisions, inaccurate reporting, lost margin |
| Order and inventory sync errors | Overselling, underselling, delayed fulfillment, manual fixes | Lower conversion, refunds, support cost increase |
| Payment operations gaps | Failed charges, delayed retries, payout reconciliation lag | Revenue leakage, cashflow uncertainty |
| Channel sprawl | Shopify plus marketplaces plus wholesale have inconsistent logic | Operational overhead, weak forecasting confidence |
| Reactive decision making | Teams report what happened but cannot predict what to do next | Slow growth and missed sales windows |

TkTurners will address these as one connected system problem, not isolated tasks.

## 4. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) and Market Focus
### Primary ICP (Year 1)
- Geography: US-based businesses only.
- Business model: omnichannel retail brands (DTC site + one or more additional channels).
- Revenue profile: roughly `$2M-$50M` annual online revenue.
- Stack complexity: Shopify or equivalent storefront, custom or legacy ERP, one or more payment gateways, multi-channel order flow.
- Urgency trigger: leadership sees clear sales leakage or ops friction and needs measurable improvement in `<120` days.

### Economic Buyer and Delivery Stakeholders
- Economic buyer: Founder, CEO, COO, or Head of Ecommerce.
- Operational stakeholder: Ops manager, finance lead, ecommerce manager.
- Technical stakeholder: internal engineer, external ERP consultant, or agency partner.

### Exclusion Filters
- Pre-revenue stores without operational scale.
- Single-channel stores with no system complexity.
- Companies unwilling to provide data access or commit to KPI measurement windows.

## 7. Data and Predictive Intelligence Strategy
### Data Sources
- Storefront platform data (orders, carts, customer behavior).
- ERP data (inventory, fulfillment, procurement, cost basis).
- Payment gateway data (authorization, failures, retries, payouts, chargebacks).
- Channel data (marketplace order flow and channel-level performance).

### Data Architecture Direction
- Build a normalized commerce event layer across systems.
- Maintain source-of-truth mapping for orders, SKUs, and payment events.
- Use lightweight warehouse and transformation pipelines to power KPI dashboards and predictive jobs.

### Predictive Use Cases (Prioritized)
1. Sales demand forecast by SKU and channel (rolling 4-week horizon).
2. Stockout risk warning by SKU cluster.
3. Failed payment recovery prioritization.
4. Channel mix recommendations based on margin and conversion trends.

### Governance and Reliability
- Data validation checks run daily.
- Model refresh cadence: weekly or bi-weekly, based on volatility.
- Human review required before high-impact automation changes.

### Break-Even Logic
Assumptions:
- Gross contribution margin after direct delivery cost: `65%`.
- Launch-year controllable fixed cost baseline: `~$96,000` (`$8,000/month`).

Formulas:
```text
Incremental break-even revenue = controllable fixed cost / contribution margin
Incremental break-even revenue = $96,000 / 0.65 = $147,692
```

Interpretation:
- The ecommerce unit can cover incremental controllable cost early.
- Full profitability depends on payroll allocation strategy during transition from generic services to niche operation.
- Target scenario (`$750k`) is the operating plan baseline for sustainable profitability.

### C. KPI Definitions
- Conversion rate = orders / sessions.
- Average order value (AOV) = revenue / number of orders.
- Repeat purchase rate = returning customers / total purchasing customers.
- Failed payment rate = failed payment attempts / total attempts.
- Reconciliation lag = average days from transaction event to financial reconciliation.
- Forecast accuracy = 1 - ABS(actual - forecast) / actual.

```ts

  automation_name: string;
  revenue_impact_score: 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5;        // required
```

Priority scoring formula:
```text
priority_score =
  (0.40 x revenue_impact_score) +
  (0.20 x (6 - implementation_effort_score)) +
  (0.20 x data_quality_score) +
  (0.20 x process_criticality_score)
```
